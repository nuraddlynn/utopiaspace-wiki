# Announcements

<span class="utopia-announcements-scope"></span>

Use Announcements to create, publish, and manage important updates for selected users, departments, companies, or the whole organisation.

<div class="utopia-announcements-actions">
  <a href="/personal-space">Personal Space</a>
  <a href="/calendar">Calendar</a>
  <a href="/meeting-notes">Meeting Notes</a>
</div>

## Overview

<div class="utopia-announcements-grid">
  <section>
    <h3>Create Updates</h3>
    <p>Add titles, summaries, announcement types, and full content for internal communication.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h3>Target Audience</h3>
    <p>Select the company, department, or wider audience that should receive the announcement.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h3>Publish Control</h3>
    <p>Publish immediately, save as a draft, or schedule the announcement for a later date.</p>
  </section>
</div>

## Announcement Fields

<div class="utopia-announcements-table">
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">Field</div>
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">What to enter</div>

  <strong>Title</strong>
  <p>Enter a clear and concise title that explains the announcement at a glance.</p>

  <strong>Summary</strong>
  <p>Provide a short description that will appear on announcement cards or previews.</p>

  <strong>Type</strong>
  <p>Select the announcement type, such as Announcement, System Update, Event/Social, or Official Memo.</p>

  <strong>Company</strong>
  <p>Choose the company or companies that the announcement applies to.</p>

  <strong>Department</strong>
  <p>Select relevant departments when the announcement is only meant for specific teams.</p>
</div>

## Creating an Announcement

<div class="utopia-announcements-flow">
  <div>
    <strong>1</strong>
    <span>Click the Add button at the top right of the Announcements page.</span>
  </div>
  <div>
    <strong>2</strong>
    <span>Fill in the required fields, including title, summary, type, company, and department.</span>
  </div>
  <div>
    <strong>3</strong>
    <span>Add the full announcement content and upload a banner image if needed.</span>
  </div>
  <div>
    <strong>4</strong>
    <span>Use Live Preview to check how the announcement will appear before publishing.</span>
  </div>
  <div>
    <strong>5</strong>
    <span>Save as Draft, Submit, or schedule the announcement depending on the publishing plan.</span>
  </div>
</div>

## Publishing Options

<div class="utopia-announcements-table">
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">Option</div>
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">Behaviour</div>

  <strong>Publish Now</strong>
  <p>The announcement is published immediately after submission.</p>

  <strong>Schedule</strong>
  <p>Set a specific date and time for the announcement to be published automatically.</p>

  <strong>Save as Draft</strong>
  <p>Keep the announcement unpublished while you review or complete the content.</p>

  <strong>Submit</strong>
  <p>Publish or send the announcement forward based on the configured workflow.</p>
</div>

## Banner and Content

<div class="utopia-announcements-grid">
  <section>
    <h3>Banner Image</h3>
    <p>Upload a JPG, PNG, or WebP banner to make the announcement visually clear. Recommended size is 1200 x 600.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h3>Full Content</h3>
    <p>Enter complete details in the content editor and format the text clearly for readers.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h3>Supporting Files</h3>
    <p>Attach supporting documents when the announcement needs extra reference material.</p>
  </section>
</div>

## Tips

<div class="utopia-announcements-table">
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">Tip</div>
  <div class="utopia-announcements-head">Why it matters</div>

  <strong>Use clear language</strong>
  <p>Readers should understand the update quickly without needing extra explanation.</p>

  <strong>Keep the summary short</strong>
  <p>A concise summary makes announcement cards easier to scan.</p>

  <strong>Check the audience</strong>
  <p>Make sure the correct company and department are selected before publishing.</p>

  <strong>Preview first</strong>
  <p>Use the preview to catch layout, typo, or formatting issues before submission.</p>
</div>
